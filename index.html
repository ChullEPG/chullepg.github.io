Chris Hull

Engineering Science Doctoral Researcher and Consultant

Chris Hull is an Energy Systems engineer affiliated with the Energy and Power Group (EPG) at the University of Oxford, where he researches the electrification of public transportation systems in Sub-Saharan Africa under Professor Malcolm McCulloch. He is also a Stanford University trained Environmental Economist, focused on achieving net-zero in an economically feasible way.

Chris has conducted engineering and economic research on four different continents since 2018, and looks forward to continuing to work on the world's most pressing problems with the world's brightest minds. Chris is excited about possibilities for new technologies to drive emerging value chains. and is interested in opportunities to join high impact energy ventures.

In addition to his research, Chris is experienced in business development, project management, and data analysis. He has provided support in these capacities for renewable energy, HVAC, and transportation start-ups, and is available for hire as a private consultant. He is also available for speaking engagements and guest lectures, and has provided college counseling since 2017. He works with a maximum of three students at a time, and currently has one open slot.

Current Work

Research

Chris is currently researching technical and economic challenges associated with public transport electrification in the developing world in his PhD thesis "Technoeconomic assessment of paratransit in Sub-Saharan Africa". He is in partnership with Stellenbosch University in the development of the first commercial battery electric retrofit for minibus taxis, which are the main mode of transport in sub-Saharan Africa.

Consulting

Experienced in business development, project management, and data analysis, Chris is available for hire as a private management/strategy consultant.

Teaching

Chris is passionate about helping students get into the college of their dreams. He works with a maximum of three students at a time, and currently has one open slot. In addition to college counseling, Chris has three years of experience teaching engineering, economics, mathematics, business & entrepreneurship at a high school and university level, and is available for guest lectures and speaking engagements.
